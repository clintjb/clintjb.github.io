#### The Nuts and Bolts (Without Boring You)

Here’s how we approached it with our Spotify preservation project:

1. **Metadata First**: Built a database with 256 million tracks – nearly everything Spotify’s ever hosted. To give you perspective, that’s over 186 million unique ISRC codes (those musical fingerprints you never think about). More identifiers than MusicBrainz has by an order of magnitude.

2. **The 99.6% Rule**: Archived 86 million actual music files covering 99.6% of all listens. If you randomly played any track on Spotify tomorrow, there’s essentially only a 0.4% chance it wouldn’t be in this collection. We prioritized using Spotify’s own popularity metric, then dove deep into the long tail.

3. **Practical Quality**: Kept it lean (of course). For popular tracks, original OGG Vorbis at 160kbit/s with embedded metadata. For deep cuts? Efficient OGG Opus at 75kbit/s – still sounds great to mortal ears, while keeping storage manageable. Could we have chased FLAC perfection? Sure. But then we’d need data centers instead of distributed torrents.

4. **Open Kitchen Approach**: Everything’s packaged in our Anna’s Archive Containers format. No gatekeeping, no proprietary nonsense. Just bulk torrents anyone can mirror if they’ve got the disk space (admittedly, “just” 300TB worth). The metadata alone is under 200GB compressed – practically bite-sized in data terms.
